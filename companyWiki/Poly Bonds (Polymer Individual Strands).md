# What Polymer Bonds Are

**Physical Description:** Individual hair extension strands with a pre-attached polymer bond tip at the root end. The bond is a clear, flat square measuring 1/4" x 1/4", resembling a small translucent tile.
The bond serves as the attachment mechanism that will be heated and molded during installation to secure the extension to natural hair.
**Polymer Bond Composition:** The bond is made from **clear polyamide hot melt adhesive** - a hot melt that forms an initial bond immediately upon cooling. This is NOT keratin despite common industry terminology.
Custom formulated for:
*   **Strength:** Secure hold for 3-4 months minimum
*   **Endurance:** High resistance to heat, conditioners, and oils
*   **Fast application:** Easy and quick to apply
*   **Clean removal:** Breaks down with alcohol-based removers
**Why Polymer (Not Keratin):** Industry commonly calls these "keratin bonds" but ours are polymer-based because:
*   Polyamide adhesive provides superior strength and flexibility
*   Better resistance to products and environmental factors
*   Cleaner, easier removal without oily residue
*   Consistent performance across all conditions

# How Polymer Bonds Are Installed

### **Tools Required:**
*   **Fusion Heat Connector:** Specialized tool with two heated prongs (set to 170-180°C / 340-360°F)
*   **Sectioning Tools:** Clips and combs for proper hair isolation
*   **Optional:** Pliers for shaping bonds
### **Installation Process:**

*   **Step 1 - Prepare Hair:** Clean hair thoroughly and dry completely. All surfaces must be clean, dry, and free of oils and residues. For oily scalps, use isopropyl alcohol during prep - spray onto root areas when hair is nearly dry, then finish drying.
*   **Step 2 - Section Natural Hair:** Isolate small section of client's natural hair appropriate to extension density. Section size critical - too much or too little causes problems.
*   **Step 3 - Position Extension:** Place poly tip UNDER the natural hair strands (easier than placing on top). Position where you want the bond to sit.
*   **Step 4 - Heat and Melt:** Pinch and hold closed the heating iron over the poly tip until bond is softened enough to mold. The polymer has a fast open time - won't burn or stick to fingers, yet remains rubbery long enough to shape.
*   **Step 5 - Shape Bond:** Pinch and roll from top to bottom, forming the bond around natural hair. Pinch hard at top and bottom for maximum strength. Apply firm pressure - hot melt adhesives are PSA (Pressure Sensitive Adhesives), so more pressure = stronger bond.
*   **Step 6 - Allow to Set:** Bond achieves 50% strength after 1 minute, 75% after 1 hour, and 100% after 24 hours. Avoid pulling, bending, or conditioning within first 24 hours.
### **Installation Tips:**
*   Avoid bonds longer than 1/2 inch
*   Can trim top of tip for shorter bond (don't trim sides)
*   Don't reheat and move bond numerous times (reduces strength)
*   When melting, don't leave un-melted globules
*   New polymer can withstand direct hot air from dryers without softening

# Installation Advantages

*   **Fast Application:** Poly bond installation is significantly faster than I-tips. Full-head installation typically takes 3-4 hours vs. 6-8+ hours for I-tips. Each piece requires: position-heat-shape process (much faster than loop-thread-position-flatten of I-tips).
*   **Less Physical Toll:** The heating and shaping motion is less repetitive and physically demanding than the constant plier squeezing required for I-tips. Stylists experience less hand fatigue and arthritis risk. Upright working position (no extreme hunching required) reduces back strain compared to I-tip precision work.
*   **Easier to Learn:** The heat-melt-shape technique is more intuitive than I-tip threading. New stylists master poly bonds faster than cylinder installations. Mistakes are more forgiving - if bond isn't perfect, it can be reheated and reshaped (though not excessively).
*   **Natural Appearance:** Individual strand placement creates natural, undetectable results. Each piece positioned for optimal blending, similar to I-tips but faster to achieve. The clear polymer bond is virtually invisible against most hair colors, especially when properly shaped flat.

# Lifespan and Maintenance

**Hold Time: 3-4 Months Minimum** When applied as directed, polymer bonds hold securely for 4-6 months. This is industry-standard for fusion extensions.
**Factors Affecting Lifespan:**
*   **Application Quality:** Proper temperature, pressure, shaping = longer hold
*   **Client Care:** Gentle handling, appropriate products = extended lifespan
*   **Oil Exposure:** Avoid long-term contact with oil/alcohol heavy products (polymer is resistant but err on caution)
*   **Hair Growth Rate:** Fast-growing hair may need earlier replacement
**Not Reusable:** Unlike I-tips, polymer bonds are single-use. Once removed, the bond is destroyed and extensions must be replaced with new bonds.
This makes poly bonds more expensive per year than I-tips, but faster installation and lower stylist physical toll offset this cost.

# Removal Process

### **Tools Needed:**
*   **Alcohol-Based Remover:** Acetone, isopropyl alcohol, or commercial extension remover
*   **Grooved Pliers:** To break and remove bonds
### **Removal Steps:**

*   **Step 1 - Break Bond:** Use pliers to crack/break the polymer bond. The bond will not simply slide off - it must be broken first.
*   **Step 2 - Apply Remover:** Saturate broken bond thoroughly with alcohol-based remover. Wait at least 30 seconds for remover to penetrate and break down polymer.
*   **Step 3 - Peel Off:** After remover has worked, peel bond away from natural hair. The polymer breaks down cleanly without oily residue.
*   **Step 4 - Clean Hair:** Remove any remaining bond residue with additional remover application. The alcohol-based formula evaporates cleanly, leaving hair ready for new installation.

**Why Our Remover Works:** Alcohol-based removers break down the polymer adhesive effectively without leaving sticky or gummy residue. The remover evaporates, so hair turns clear again rather than remaining coated.
**Not Sticky or Gummy:** Our polymer bonds are NOT sticky when removing (unlike some competitors). Once remover is applied, bond crumbles with plier assistance rather than creating messy, gummy situations.

# Application Technical Details

*   **Temperature:** 170-180°C (340-360°F) for optimal melting and sealing. This temperature range provides:
    *   Fast open time (doesn't burn fingers)
    *   Sufficient working time (remains pliable for shaping)
    *   Strong final bond
*   **Open Time:** Fast open time means bond won't burn or stick to fingers, yet remains rubbery long enough for shaping. This balance makes application easier and safer.
*   **Surface Preparation:** All surfaces must be clean, dry, free of oils and residues. For oily scalps:
    *   Use isopropyl alcohol during prep
    *   Spray onto root areas when hair nearly dry
    *   Finish drying completely before application
    *   **Bond Strength Development:**
    *   **1 minute:** 50% strength
    *   **1 hour:** 75% strength
    *   **24 hours:** 100% strength (optimum)
*   **Pressure = Strength:** Hot melt adhesives are PSA (Pressure Sensitive Adhesives). More pressure during application = stronger bond. Give top and bottom of bond extra hard pinch directly after shaping.
*   **Heat Resistance:** New polymer formula can withstand direct hot air from hair dryers without softening. Clients can use normal heat styling without bond compromise.

# Common Questions

*   **Will the bond turn white?"**
*   **"Can client wash hair after applying?"**
    *   Wait 1-2 days before washing. Bonds get stronger over time and reach optimum strength after 24 hours.
    
*   **"How difficult is removal?"**
    *   Not difficult with proper remover and pliers. Bonds are not sticky or gummy - they crumble cleanly when broken down with alcohol-based remover.
    
*   **"Can bonds be reused?"**
    *   No. Polymer bonds are single-use. Once removed, bond is destroyed and new extensions must be installed.
    
*   **"What if I need a shorter bond?"**
    *   Trim top of tip for shorter bond length, but don't trim sides (weakens bond structure).
    
*   **"Do I need special remover?"**
    *   Most extension removers work. Even drugstore isopropyl alcohol or nail polish remover (acetone) coupled with grooved pliers will break down bonds effectively.

# Tips for Success

### **Avoid These Mistakes:**
*   Bonds longer than 1/2 inch (too bulky, visible)
*   Trimming bond sides (weakens structure)
*   Reheating and moving bond numerous times (reduces strength)
*   Leaving un-melted globules (creates weak spots)
*   Conditioning within first 24 hours (bond still hardening)
### **Best Practices:**
*   Position tip UNDER hair strands (easier to pinch)
*   Apply firm pressure when shaping (PSA requires pressure)
*   Pinch hard at top and bottom (maximum strength points)
*   Work methodically (rushed work creates poor bonds)
*   Keep bonds flat and smooth (comfortable for client)
**Storage:** Keep sealed in zip-lock bag in cool, dry place when not in use. Prevents moisture absorption and maintains bond integrity.
