# Official Names & Industry Terminology

**Our Name: Cylinder** Based on the cylindrical shape of the copper link used for attachment. Clear and descriptive naming.
### **Common Industry Names (All refer to the same product):**
*   **I-Tip:** Most common term, referring to the "I" shape of the link's opening
*   **Microlink:** Emphasizes the small "micro" size of individual links
*   **Microbead:** Sometimes used interchangeably (though "bead" might suggest round vs. cylindrical)
**Use Any Term Interchangeably:** Our team recognizes all terminology variations. We meet customers where they are with the language they know.

# What Cylinder I-Tips Are

### **Physical Description:**
*   Individual hair strands
*   Cylinder flattens inside a small copper tube during installation to secure extension to natural hair

* * *

# I-Tip Construction Technology

**Each strand is encapsulated within a clear abrasion-resistant tube through a specialized process:**
1. **Root bonding:** First, root strands are bonded with polyamide hot melt adhesive
2. **Tube sealing:** Then the tube is sealed to create a protective sheath
3. **Hand-shaped:** All tips are made by hand and shaped to fit into our Cylinder copper links
### **Finely Engineered Tips:**
*   Will NOT crack or shed
*   Can be re-attached numerous times
*   Remain strong and flexible throughout lifespan
**Aerospace Industry Technology:** Our tips are protected with a special sheath originally produced for the aerospace industry, providing:
*   Long-lasting strength
*   Maintained flexibility
*   Superior durability compared to standard I-tips
This advanced construction is what makes our cylinder I-tips truly reusable and long-lasting.

# How Cylinder I-Tips Are Installed

### **Tools Required:**
*   **Loop Tool:** Threaded through cylinder to pull natural hair through link
*   **Pliers:** Extension-specific pliers to flatten cylinder without crushing completely
*   **Sectioning Tools:** Clips and combs for proper sectioning
### **Installation Process:**
1. **Prepare:** Thread metal link onto loop tool
2. **Section:** Isolate small section of natural hair (section size is critical)
3. **Thread Natural Hair:** Insert loop tool through link, catch hair, pull through cylinder
4. **Add Extension:** Thread extension tip through link with natural hair
5. **Position:** Slide link to correct position (not too close to scalp, not too far down)
6. **Flatten:** Use pliers to flatten cylinder, compressing evenly to secure both hairs together

## **Installation Challenges:**

### **Extremely Time-Intensive:**
*   Full-head installation: 6-8+ hours
*   Each strand requires complete loop-thread-position-flatten process
*   100-300+ individual pieces for full head
*   "It took me a minute just to demonstrate one correctly" - multiply by hundreds
### **Requires Precision:**
*   Every single installation must be executed precisely
*   Slight positioning errors (too high, low, tight, loose) = uncomfortable/visible/problematic
*   No shortcuts - each piece demands full attention
*   Rushing creates poor results clients immediately feel/see
### **Loop-Thread-Position-Flatten Cycle:**
*   Repetitive cycle becomes mentally and physically exhausting over hours
*   Tedious repetition why many stylists avoid I-tips
*   Mental stamina required to maintain precision through hundreds of repetitions
### **Physical Toll on Stylists:**
*   **Arthritis Development:** "A lot of women with arthritis—really bad arthritis—have gotten it from doing this for years. It messes up their hands entirely."
*   **Back Problems:** "Their backs too. They're always hunched over, not paying attention to posture."
*   **Unsustainability:** Many stylists forced to stop due to physical damage - literally unsustainable over extended careers
**Immediate Restart if Errors:** If positioning/flattening is incorrect, entire piece must be removed and restarted completely. No quick fix = mistakes waste significant time.

# Large Tip vs Small Tip

**CRITICAL: "Tip" size refers to amount of HAIR per piece, AND tip size.**
### Large Tip
*   **Pieces per Bundle:** 25 strands
*   **Hair Density:** MORE hair per strand
*   **Weight per Strand:** 0.7g-0.8g
*   **Total Bundle Weight:** 17g for 16" & 18" and 20g for 20" & 22"
*   **Use Case:** Normal to thick hair, standard installations, desired volume
*   **Color Availability:** Due to low sales, only popular colors are available
### Small Tip
*   **Pieces per Bundle:** 50 strands
*   **Hair Density:** LESS hair per strand
*   **Weight per Strand:** 0.3g
*   **Total Bundle Weight:** 17g for 16" & 18" (only avaiable in these length)
*   **Use Case:** Fine/thin hair, thinning hair, minimal stress, delicate areas
*   **Color Availability:** Popular colors only in 16" & 18" only (limited selection)
**Bundle Weight Consistency:** Both weigh same total (17g). Difference is distribution - Large tip = 17g in 25 thicker pieces; Small tip = 17g in 50 thinner pieces.

# Lifespan and Reusability

**Lifespan: 1-2 Years** Properly cared for I-tips last 1-2 years depending on:
*   **Client Care:** Gentle handling, appropriate products, minimal heat, proper washing
*   **Installation Quality:** Well-executed by skilled stylist
*   **Hair Growth Rate:** Fast growth = more frequent move-ups = more wear
*   **Maintenance Schedule:** Regular move-ups every 6-12 months maintain optimal positioning
**Fully Reusable:** I-tips can be removed, cleaned, and reinstalled multiple times. The aerospace sheath technology ensures tips remain strong and flexible through multiple installations.
### **Reusability Process:**
1. Remove extensions (open/break links)
2. Clean tips and links
3. Reinstall with same or different links
4. Tips designed for multiple re-attachments without cracking or shedding
### **Cost Efficiency:**
*   Initial investment: $800-1200
*   If lasting 3 years with 2 move-ups/year = $22-33/month
*   Compare to poly bonds at $800 every 3-4 months = $200-267/month
*   **I-tips cost 1/8th as much per month**

# Removal Process

### **Tools Needed:**
*   Pliers (same ones used for installation)
*   Opening tool (optional, makes removal easier)
### **Removal Steps:**
1. **Locate Link:** Find flattened metal link
2. **Open Link:** Use pliers to open/break flattened link
3. **Slide Off:** Once opened, slide off natural hair with extension
4. **Collect Links:** Save removed links if planning to reuse (clean before storage)
### **No Chemical Removers Needed:**
*   Purely mechanical removal
*   Zero chemicals or solvents required
*   Gentle on natural hair (no chemical stress or allergic reactions)
**Clean Removal Result:** Properly executed removal leaves natural hair clean without residue, buildup, or remaining attachment materials.

# Current Market Status

**Declining Extension Type:** "I would say cylinder is a dying extension type, for sure." Becoming progressively less common in American market despite quality advantages.
### **Why Declining in America:**
1. **Time Investment:** 6-8 hours conflicts with "I want it now" culture
2. **Stylist Physical Toll:** Arthritis/back problems deter new stylists
3. **Faster Alternatives:** Poly bonds = similar results in half the time; wefts even faster
4. **Marketing Challenges:** Don't photograph dramatically for social media
5. **Client Awareness:** Fewer know to request vs. "what Kardashians use"
### **Still Popular in Europe:**
Ukraine and France maintain strong I-tip markets. Different cultural values:
*   Appreciate durability and traditional craftsmanship
*   Different labor costs/pricing structures
*   Established tradition creates self-perpetuating customer bases
*   Less celebrity-influenced
### **Our Inventory Response:**
*   Reduced I-tip color inventory from comprehensive to focused on popular colors
*   Discontinued slow-selling colors
*   Empty inventory bins demonstrate reduction
*   Still maintain large tip in popular colors, small tip in most-requested shades
*   Custom orders possible for unusual combinations with lead time
