# Specialty Colors & Custom Blends

_Verified by Elizabeth & Nicole_

# Gray White Hair

**What It Is:** Natural salt-and-pepper hair from donors whose hair has naturally turned gray/white. Collected from temple donations, undergoes only bleach washing (not dyeing) to remove residual pigment and create uniform brightness.
### **Key Characteristics:**
*   Practically virgin hair - healthiest we offer (minimal processing)
*   Natural tone variation (pure white, silver-gray, darker gray = authentic salt-and-pepper)
*   Our rarest offering due to sourcing challenges (limited stock)
*   Most expensive color due to rarity and premium condition
### **Best For:**
*   Natural gray transition (embracing silver hair gracefully)
*   Trendy silver look without bleaching natural hair
*   Foundation for custom gray blend orders
*   Most authentic gray appearance (real gray vs. toned blonde)
### **Availability:**
*   All extension types (I-tips, poly bonds, wefts)
*   All textures (natural wave, extra body wave, curly)
*   Limited stock (inquire before ordering)

# Ash White

**What It Is:** Gray White hair processed through our cold dye process to create pure, uniform white with cool ash tones. No yellow, warmth, or gray variation.
**Development:** Created ~2 years ago as extension of Level 12 Ash innovation. Same cold dye technology that enabled superior ash tones enabled Ash White creation.
### **Key Characteristics:**
*   Pure white throughout (no tonal variation)
*   Industry rarity (few companies can achieve true white without destroying integrity)
*   No yellow tones, resists yellowing over time
*   Uniform consistency (vs. Gray White's natural variation)
### **Best For:**
*   Pure white fashion color (not blonde, not gray, but actual white)
*   Icy white aesthetic with maximum brightness
*   Those fighting brassiness

# Gray White vs. Ash White

| Feature | Gray White | Ash White |
| ---| ---| --- |
| Appearance | Natural tonal variation, salt-and-pepper | Uniform pure white, no variation |
| Processing | Minimally processed (bleach wash only) | Cold dye processed |
| Effect | Authentic aging appearance | Dramatic white statement |
| Best For | Natural gray matching, maximum health | Fashion-forward pure white, uniform color |

**Both can be mixed** as bases for custom gray blend orders.

# Rooted Shades (Two-Tone Color)

**What They Are:** Darker color at root transitioning to lighter color through length/ends. Creates intentional "grown-out" appearance with seamless gradual blend (not harsh lines).
### **Benefits:**
1. **Natural regrowth appearance** - Actual regrowth blends with designed dark root
2. **Low maintenance** - Natural regrowth expected and intentional
3. **Long-term savings** - Fewer color touch-ups needed
### **Customization:**
*   Any two colors (root must be darker than body)
*   Root depth can be specified (2-3" vs. 5-6" root zones)
*   Transition blending style (subtle gradual vs. more defined)
*   Custom order required (4-6 weeks, clear specifications needed)

# Blended Shades (Piano Pattern)

**What They Are:** Two colors mixed throughout in "piano key" alternating pattern. Creates highlight/lowlight effects without coloring natural hair.
**Purpose:** Achieve dimensional color WITHOUT damaging natural hair through bleaching. Extensions provide color dimension while preserving virgin hair health.
### **Applications:**
*   **Highlight effects:** Level 4 Natural + Level 7 Natural = caramel highlights in brown
*   **Dimensional color:** Multiple tones create visual interest, fuller appearance
*   **Experimentation:** Try colors temporarily without permanent commitment
### **Customization:**
*   **Custom percentages:** Full blend or Partial Blend.

# Gray Blends (Most Popular Specialty)

**What They Are:** Gray White OR Ash White mixed with any single color (Levels 1-12, any shade). Creates salt-and-pepper effects, dimensional gray transitions, or lightened blonde.
### **Base Options:**
*   **Gray White base:** Natural tonal variation, authentic aging
*   **Ash White base:** Uniform white tones, controlled appearance
**Mix With Any Color:** Unlimited pairing flexibility. Common combinations:
*   Gray White + Level 4 Natural = salt-and-pepper brown
*   Ash White + Level 12 Ash = platinum blonde with white
*   Gray White + Level 7 Ash = dimensional gray-brown
### Standard Percentages

| Gray Level | Description |
| ---| --- |
| 25% Gray | Subtle gray dimension, color remains dominant<br>Perfect for beginning gray transition or trendy silver accent |
| 50% Gray | True salt-and-pepper, equal amounts<br>Authentic middle-aged gray or fashion-forward silver dimension |
| 75% Gray | Predominantly gray with color accent for depth<br>Mostly gray natural hair matching |

**Custom Percentages:** Any percentage available (80%, 60%, 40%, 30%, etc.) for precise matching or specific aesthetics. Can adjust over time as natural gray increases (25% → 40% → 60% progression).
### Popular Uses
1. **Transitioning to gray naturally** - Match increasing gray percentage over time
2. **Lightening blonde appearance** - Gray White + Level 12 Bleach = dramatically lighter than Level 12 alone
3. **Salt-and-pepper effect** - Sophisticated at any age
4. **Adding dimension to blonde** - White mixed with blonde = dimensional platinum with depth
### Visual Impact
Nicole's demonstration: Level 12 Bleach vs. Level 12 Bleach + 25% Gray White shows dramatic difference. Gray blend is noticeably lighter, brighter, more impactful.
**Why it works:** White reflects light more intensely than even lightest blonde. Adding white increases overall light reflection, creates movement and shimmer.
### Considerations
**Maintenance:**
*   Gray/white can show brassiness (requires purple shampoo)
*   Ash White needs less maintenance than Gray White (starts cooler)
*   Shows regrowth more obviously than darker colors (high contrast)
**Best For:** Clients who already have natural gray hair (seamless blending). Non-gray clients must understand regrowth visibility.

# Ordering Specialty Colors
