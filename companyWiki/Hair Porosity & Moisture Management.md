# Hair Porosity & Moisture Management

_Verified by Elizabeth & Nicole_

# What is Hair Porosity?

**Definition:** Hair porosity refers to the hair's ability to absorb and retain moisture, determined by the cuticle condition (outer protective layer). It explains why the same product works differently for different people and dictates which products and techniques will be effective.
### **Three Porosity Types:**
*   **Low Porosity:** Cuticles tightly closed - moisture struggles to enter but stays in once absorbed
*   **Medium Porosity:** Cuticles balanced - moisture enters and retains at normal, healthy rates
*   **High Porosity:** Cuticles widely open - moisture enters easily but escapes just as easily

**Important:** Not all hair is created equal. Every person's natural hair and every batch of extensions can have different porosity levels due to natural variation in donor hair genetics and processing history.

* * *

# Low Porosity Hair

### **Characteristics:**
*   Cuticles tightly closed, flat against hair shaft
*   Water beads on surface rather than absorbing
*   Takes very long to get fully wet
*   Products sit on top creating buildup
*   Won't hold styles easily
*   Takes long to dry once wet
*   Often healthy, undamaged hair
### **Care Requirements:**
*   **Clarifying is ESSENTIAL** every 4-6 weeks minimum to remove inevitable buildup
*   Use lightweight, liquid, water-based products (not heavy oils/butters)
*   Apply gentle heat to open cuticles temporarily (warm water, steaming, hooded dryer)
*   Avoid heavy oils (castor, coconut) and butters (shea, cocoa)
**Reality:** This is often healthy hair that's difficult to style. The "nothing works" frustration stems from structural porosity, not product choice. Once you understand the techniques needed, management becomes possible.
* * *

# Medium Porosity Hair

### **Characteristics:**
*   Cuticles balanced with slight spacing for controlled moisture entry
*   Absorbs moisture at steady, normal rate
*   Retains moisture for several days
*   Products absorb well and deliver intended benefits
*   Holds styles easily
*   Color takes evenly
### **Care Requirements:**
*   Standard hair care products work well
*   Regular shampooing sufficient (clarify only every 1-2 months)
*   Balanced moisture routine: shampoo, condition, occasional deep conditioning (1-2x monthly)
*   Most straightforward, predictable maintenance

**Reality:** This is the ideal "Goldilocks zone" - the goal state everyone hopes for. Most user-friendly, easiest client experience, most predictable results.

* * *

# High Porosity Hair

### **Characteristics:**
*   Cuticles raised, lifted, widely spaced with visible gaps
*   Rapidly absorbs moisture but loses it just as quickly
*   Chronically dry regardless of product application
*   Extremely prone to frizz (worse in humidity)
*   Rough, coarse, "straw-like" texture in severe cases
*   Tangles easily (cuticles act like Velcro)
*   Feels dry even after conditioning
### **Common Causes:**
*   Most curly hair naturally has higher porosity due to curl structure
*   Bleached/color-treated hair (Levels 10-12 almost always high porosity)
*   Heat damage from excessive styling
*   Chemical treatments (perms, relaxers, keratin)
*   Over-processed hair with multiple damage sources

### **Care Requirements - "Moisture is EVERYTHING":**

*   **Constant moisture replenishment** - daily application necessary ("all the time, like all the time")
*   Product layering: leave-in conditioner + curl cream + oil + anti-frizz serum
*   Wash only 2-3x per week maximum
*   Co-wash (conditioner-only) between shampoos
*   Clarify every 2-3 weeks (more frequent than other types due to heavy product use)
*   Follow clarifying immediately with intensive deep conditioning
### **Required Products:**
*   Moisturizing shampoo
*   Low pH conditioner
*   Leave-in conditioner (essential)
*   Curl cream (for textured hair)
*   Anti-frizz products

* * *

## CRITICAL RULE: NEVER Brush Curly Hair When Dry

**Why:** Brushing dry curly high porosity hair instantly destroys curl definition and creates massive frizz ("you're gonna be back in the '70s"). The raised cuticles catch on brushes and each other, creating exponential frizz with each stroke.
**When to Detangle:** ONLY when soaking wet with conditioner/product applied. Start from ends, work toward roots. Use wide-tooth combs or fingers, never force through tangles.
**Styling Method:** Use scrunching (cup hair in palms, squeeze upward toward scalp) instead of brushing to encourage curl formation without disruption.

## High Porosity Reality Check

*   **Constant work** - genuinely requires daily attention, never becomes easy
*   **Always fighting to maintain** - ongoing battle against moisture loss, frizz, tangling
*   **Many give up** - eventually resort to heat straightening for simplicity
*   **Not everyone can commit** - assess honestly before purchasing curly extensions
*   **Worth it for committed clients** - unique beauty when properly maintained

## Porosity in Our Extensions

**Blonde Extensions (Levels 11-12):** Almost always higher porosity due to extensive bleaching required. This is unavoidable - you cannot achieve very light colors without some porosity increase.
**Why 11 Ash and Any Level 12 Curly is Impossible:** Bleaching required would create such severe high porosity that curl patterns would be destroyed entirely. The cuticle damage would be too extensive to maintain curl formation.
**Color Effect on Curl:** Bleaching increases porosity and thins cuticles, causing curls to relax and appear looser. Level 11 bleach curly looks notably less curly than Level 2 curly from the same source.

## Explaining Porosity to Customers

### **Common Complaints Indicating Porosity Issues:**
*   "This hair feels different than my last set"
*   "Why won't it hold a curl?"
*   "It's so dry"
*   "It tangles all the time"
**Educational Response:** "Not all hair is created equal. Each batch has different porosity—how it absorbs and holds moisture. Let me help you understand your hair's porosity and what it needs."
### **Approach:**
1. Explain porosity concepts
2. Help identify their porosity level (water test)
3. Provide specific care recommendations tailored to that level
4. Frame variations as evidence of authenticity: "Natural hair means natural variations. This is the nature of working with 100% natural, unprocessed hair."
This transforms complaints into learning opportunities and provides actionable solutions rather than defensive explanations.
