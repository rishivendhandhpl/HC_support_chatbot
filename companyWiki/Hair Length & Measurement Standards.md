# Hair Length & Measurement Standards

_Verified by Elizabeth & Nicole_

# How We Measure: Stretched State
# (Universal Standard)

**ALL extensions are measured in stretched state** - hair pulled completely straight with tension applied, eliminating any curl, wave, or natural contraction. This is the universal industry standard.
**Why:** Provides the only fair and consistent way to compare across different textures and brands.
**Critical Customer Understanding:** The listed length (16", 20", etc.) is stretched measurement, NOT how hair appears when worn naturally. This distinction is essential for wavy and curly textures.

* * *

# Stretched vs. Relaxed Length

**Stretched Length:** Hair's measurement when pulled completely straight (technical specification)
**Relaxed Length:** How hair appears when worn naturally in its textured state (visual appearance)
**The Gap = Shrinkage:** The apparent length loss when texture forms
**Most Common Complaint (100% Preventable):** "I ordered 20 inches but it only looks 18 inches"
*   The hair IS exactly 20 inches when measured correctly (stretched)
*   Customer was visualizing relaxed appearance, not stretched measurement
*   Solution: Education BEFORE purchase

* * *

# Shrinkage by Texture

| Texture | Shrinkage | Example |
| ---| ---| --- |
| Natural Wave | ~1 inch | 16" stretched = ~15" relaxed |
| Extra Body Wave | 1-2 inches | 16" stretched = ~14-15" relaxed |
| Curly | 2-3 inches | 16" stretched = ~14-14.5" relaxed |

**Important:** Shrinkage increases with length. While percentage stays ~15-20%, absolute inches increase:
*   14" curly = 2" shrinkage
*   26" curly = 3-4" shrinkage

# Customer Education Requirements
# (NON-OPTIONAL)

### **MUST inform EVERY curly and wavy customer BEFORE purchase about:**
1. Stretched vs. relaxed measurement difference
2. Expected shrinkage for their texture
3. How to order for desired appearance
### **How We Communicate:**
*   Product pages: Clear statements about stretched measurement
*   Order confirmations: Reminder about shrinkage and expected appearance
*   Customer service: Always explain during inquiries
*   YouTube videos: Visual demonstrations of stretched vs. relaxed
**Visual Demonstration is Essential:** Nicole's example shows 16" curly at barely 14.5" relaxed, then extends to exactly 16" when pulled straight. Seeing this difference prevents complaints better than verbal explanation.

# How to Order for Desired Appearance

**The Challenge:** Customers think "I want hair to LOOK 20 inches" but we measure in stretched length.
**Solution - Ask the Right Question:** "How long do you want the hair to LOOK when worn naturally?" (not "What length do you want to order?")
**Then Guide to Correct Order:**
*   Desired 18" curly appearance? → Order 20" stretched
*   Desired 20" curly appearance? → Order 22-23" stretched
**Landing Length (Custom Orders):** For custom curly orders, specify "landing length" = desired appearance when worn naturally
*   "Needs to LAND at 20 inches" = production creates 22-23" stretched to achieve 20" appearance
*   Production calculates backward accounting for shrinkage
*   Ensures customer gets visual result they want

# Available Lengths by Texture

### **Natural Wave:**
*   All standard lengths available
*   Minimal shrinkage = what they order ≈ what they see
### **Extra Body Wave:**
*   All standard lengths available
*   Account for 1-2" shrinkage
### **Curly:**
*   Standard stock: 12-20" stretched
*   Custom orders: Sometimes up to 22" (not guaranteed, depends on source hair availability)
*   Level 11-12: Extremely limited or unavailable in longer lengths
### **Why Longer Curly is Difficult:**
1. Natural growth limitations (curly hair rarely grows extremely long before breaking)
2. Processing challenges (longer = more bleaching = destroyed curl pattern)
3. Quality maintenance (very long curly accumulates environmental damage)

* * *

# Weft Length Considerations

### **Hand-tied wefts require extra source hair:**
*   Root return construction consumes 2-3" before usable weft length begins
*   30" finished weft needs ~33" source hair
### **Curly Wefts = Double Impact:**
*   Construction waste (3") + Curl shrinkage (2-3") = Need extremely long source hair
*   26" curly weft might require 30+" source hair
*   This makes long curly wefts rare, expensive, custom-order only with no guarantee

* * *

# Length and Texture Relationship (Weight Effect)

**Physics:** Longer hair weighs more → weight pulls down texture → appears looser and more relaxed
### **Examples:**
*   14" curly shows tight ringlets
*   26" curly appears almost wavy due to weight
*   Same curl pattern, different weight
### **Premium Finish Effect:**
*   Premium (70% double drawn) = dramatic weight increase
*   Premium natural wave looks much straighter than layered
*   Premium extra body wave loses root-to-tip body
*   Premium curly would destroy curl definition (that's why we don't offer it)
**Customer Expectation Management:** Very long textured hair won't show same texture intensity as shorter lengths. Better to order shorter length with desired texture than longer length with disappointing texture relaxation.

# Quick Reference: Common Questions

*   **"Why does this look shorter?"**
    *   Shrinkage misunderstanding. Show stretched vs. relaxed demonstration.
    
*   **"Can I order shorter than standard?"**
    *   Yes (custom), but curly needs 12+" for ringlet formation, EBW needs 10+" for full waves.
    
*   **"Will it grow?"**
    *   No. Extensions are cut hair that never grow. Order longer initially if you want longer appearance later.
    
*   **"Should I order longer for growth?"**
    *   Depends on installation:
        *   Wefts (6-month move-ups): Order slightly longer for natural hair growth
        *   Poly bonds (3-4 month replacement): Match current length

# Best Practices

1. **Always show visual examples** - words alone don't work for spatial concepts
2. **Confirm understanding** - have customer explain it back ("If you order 20" curly, how long will it appear?")
3. **Document education** - note on order forms that customer was informed
4. **Offer consulting** - for complex orders, guide to appropriate specifications rather than just taking orders

# Length Selection Guide

### **Short (12-16"):**
*   Best for: Volume without dramatic length, subtle enhancement
*   Texture: Maximum definition, minimal weight
### **Medium (18-22"):**
*   Best for: Noticeable length addition, most popular, versatile
*   Texture: Good definition with moderate weight, most reliable results
### **Long (24-26+"):**
*   Best for: Dramatic transformation, maximum length
*   Texture: Significant relaxation due to weight (especially curly/EBW)
*   Requires: Understanding that texture appears looser
### **Custom Extended:**
*   Best for: Very specific needs, special events
*   Reality: Not guaranteed, 4-6 weeks wait, premium pricing, requires photos and consultation
