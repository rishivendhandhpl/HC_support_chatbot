# Hair Finishes (Layered vs Premium)

_Verified by Elizabeth & Nicole_

# Understanding "Finish"

**Finish** refers to how hair is "drawn" during production—the process of aligning and sorting strands.
*   **Layered (Single Drawn):** Hair is drawn once to align the roots, keeping all natural strand lengths
*   **Premium (Double Drawn):** Hair is drawn twice—first to remove shorter lengths, then again to align roots
All our hair comes from the same high-quality Indian Remy source—the difference is in the drawing process.

# Two Finish Types

## 1\. Premium Finish (70% Double Drawn)

### **Production Process:**
*   Drawn twice: first pass removes shorter strands, second pass aligns roots
*   70% of strands are full length, hand-sorted to keep only the longest pieces
*   50% material waste (discarded shorter strands)
### **Visual Characteristics:**
*   Smooth, uniform appearance from top to almost the bottom
*   Only the bottom few inches taper to the ends
*   Blunt, thick, full ends
*   Heavier weight and substantial feel
*   Creates softer, more relaxed wave patterns due to weight
### **Best For:**
*   Clients with normal to thick hair (can support the extra weight)
*   Those wanting maximum fullness and glamorous volume
*   Clients with naturally blunt, full ends
*   Transitioning from heavily-processed brands (Great Lengths, Bellami)
*   Professional wig makers who want maximum control
### **Considerations:**
*   Costs 40-50% more due to double drawing process and 50% material waste
*   Weight pulls down and relaxes wave/curl patterns
*   May require more cutting to blend with naturally wispy ends
*   Requires strong natural hair to support the weight
### **Available In:**
*   **Natural Wave:** Fully stocked (creates subtle 2A waves)
*   **Extra Body Wave:** Custom order only (weight reduces body)
*   **Curly:** NOT AVAILABLE (weight destroys curl definition)
* * *

## 2\. Layered Finish (Single Drawn)

### **Production Process:**
*   Drawn once to align roots only
*   Keeps all naturally grown strand lengths
*   30% material waste (only damaged strands removed)
### **Visual Characteristics:**
*   Mixed strand lengths starting 5-6 inches from the roots
*   Naturally tapers to thin, wispy ends
*   Graduated, dimensional appearance like a layered haircut
*   Lighter overall weight
*   Maintains maximum texture definition (waves and curls stay pronounced)
### **Best For:**
*   Clients with fine, thin, or fragile hair (lighter weight prevents damage)
*   Those with naturally wispy, layered ends
*   Seeking natural, effortless appearance
*   Budget-conscious clients (standard pricing)
*   Maximum wave and curl definition
### **Benefits:**
*   Minimal cutting needed for blending
*   More forgiving installation
*   Natural movement and bounce
*   Gentler on natural hair
*   Less material waste = better value
### **Available In:**
*   **Natural Wave:** Fully stocked (creates fuller 2B waves)
*   **Extra Body Wave:** Fully stocked (deep beach wave, root-to-tip body)
*   **Curly:** Fully stocked, levels 1-11 (essential for curl formation and bounce)

# Texture Descriptions

### **Natural Wave (Slight to Body Wave)**
*   Premium: Subtle 2A waves, fuller along length, only bottom inches taper
*   Layered: More pronounced 2B waves with body, tapers from 5-6 inches
### **Extra Body Wave (Beach Deep Wave)**
*   Layered only: Full body wave from roots to ends, blends well with curly
*   Premium: Custom order—loses characteristic body, resembles relaxed natural wave
### **Curly (Loose Curly, 2" Curl Pattern)**
*   Layered only: Shorter lengths from 5-6" create essential bounce
*   Relaxed length: No less than 1" shorter than stated
*   Stretched length: At least 1" longer than stated
*   Available in levels 1-11 Bleach

# Common Questions

*   **"Why are there short strands in layered hair?"**
    *   The mixed lengths are intentional—they're kept during the single drawing process to create volume, movement, and natural appearance. This is the definition of single drawn, not a defect.
    
*   **"The ends look thin on layered hair."**
    *   Wispy, tapered ends are the defining characteristic of single drawn finish. They blend beautifully with naturally wispy hair and create realistic movement.
    
*   **"Is premium better than layered?"**
    *   No—they're different, not better or worse. Premium (double drawn) offers fullness; layered (single drawn) offers texture and natural movement. The best choice depends on your hair type, desired look, and budget.
    
*   **"Can I get premium curly?"**
    *   No. Premium's weight from double drawing would destroy curl definition. Curly texture requires single drawn finish to maintain bounce and curl pattern.
