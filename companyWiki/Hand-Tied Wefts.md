# Hand-Tied Wefts

_Verified by Elizabeth & Nicole_

# What Hand-Tied Wefts Are

Hand-tied wefts are extensions where hair is hand-tied onto threads, creating a continuous curtain of hair. Unlike individual strand methods (I-tips and poly bonds), wefts install as complete sections.
### Construction Method
Each weft is meticulously hand-tied—groups of strands are woven onto strong, durable threads. To ensure consistency and prevent shedding, wefts are machine-sewn across the track after hand-tying.

# Weft Track Densities

### What is Track Density?
Track density refers to how much hair is woven onto the weft track. Higher density = more hair per inch = heavier, fuller wefts.
### Our 6 Density Options
1. **Micro Track** - Ultra-lightweight, minimal density, barely detectable
2. **Fine Track** - Lightweight for fine to normal hair
3. **Thin Track** - Light density, most versatile
4. **Full Track** - Double the Thin density
5. **Thick Track** - 25% more hair than Full track
6. **Heavy Track** - 50% more density for dramatic volume & maximum density

# How Wefts Are Installed

### Two Main Installation Methods
**Bead and Sew Method:**
1. Install microbeads along horizontal sections of natural hair
2. Position weft against bead row
3. Sew weft track to beads using thread and curved needle
4. Repeat for multiple rows (typically 3-6)
**Braidless/Sewn Method:**
1. Braid small, flat cornrows along horizontal sections
2. Position weft against braid
3. Sew weft track directly to braid
4. Repeat for multiple rows
### Installation Time
Full-head installation: 3-5 hours Partial installation: 1-2 hours

# Why Wefts Are the Healthiest Method

Industry professionals widely agree that hand-tied wefts are the healthiest extension method because:
*   **No Glue**: Zero adhesive or bonding agents
*   **No Heat**: Installation requires no heat application
*   **No Metal Clamping**: No compression on individual hair strands
*   **Just Sewn**: Thread attachment is the gentlest securing method
### Distributed Weight
Weft weight distributes along the entire horizontal foundation row rather than concentrating on individual attachment points. A 30-gram weft spreads weight across hundreds of natural hairs, with each bearing a tiny fraction of the total weight.
This distribution prevents traction alopecia and reduces stress on individual follicles.
### Installation Benefits
*   Flat, seamless appearance that disappears under natural hair
*   Natural, cohesive movement
*   Efficient full coverage with 3-4 strategic rows
*   Customizable width—tracks can be cut to any desired width

# Weft Lifespan and Maintenance

### Lifespan: 1-4 Years
Hand-tied wefts last 1-4 years with proper care and maintenance.
### Move-Up Schedule: Every 6 Months
As natural hair grows, wefts require repositioning every 6 months. Move-ups involve removing wefts, creating new foundation in higher positions, and re-sewing the same wefts.
### What Gets Replaced
*   **Thread**: Replaced at each move-up
*   **Beads**: May be replaced if showing wear
*   **Wefts**: Reused for entire 1-4 year lifespan
### Move-Up Appointments
**Duration**: 2-4 hours
**Process**: Remove wefts → Remove old foundation → Create new foundation → Reinstall same wefts → Trim/style
### Removal Process
Weft removal is the simplest, gentlest removal of all extension types:
1. Cut sewing thread with scissors
2. Slide weft away from foundation
3. Remove beads or unbraid cornrows
4. Shampoo to remove thread remnants
No chemicals, no heat, no complex tools required.

### Layering Techniques
If tracks need to be shortened, use these techniques instead of cutting the track:
**Staggering**: First weft placed below second and sewn together for flat installation
**Sandwich**: Track folded into thirds and sewn together (typically for nap area)
**Fold & Stagger**: Multiple tracks staggered in center and sewn, with folded ends tapered for sides

Most stylists recommend 4-6 bundles (4-6 ounces/116-174 grams) for full head installation for length and volume, 2-3 bundles (2-3 ounces/60-90 grams) for partial installation for volume only.
