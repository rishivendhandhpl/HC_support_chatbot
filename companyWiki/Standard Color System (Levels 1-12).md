# Standard Color System (Levels 1-12)

_Verified by Elizabeth & Nicole_

# Understanding Hair Color Levels

**The Level System:** Hair color is organized on a scale from 1 (darkest) to 12 (lightest), representing depth or darkness. This universal system allows consistent communication worldwide.
### **Important Distinction:**
*   **Level** = darkness/lightness (1-12)
*   **Shade** = undertone (natural, bleach, ash, red, gold)
Both specifications are necessary for complete color identification. A customer wanting "Level 7" must also choose which shade: natural, ash, gold, or red.
**Our Range:** Complete color range from Level 1 (jet black) through Level 12 (platinum blonde). However, not all textures are available in all levels due to processing limitations.

# The Bleaching Process

**Natural Source Hair:** Arrives at Level 2 (dark brown to black) from South Indian temple donors. Any color lighter than Level 2-3 requires bleaching.
### **How We Achieve Lighter Levels:**
1. Production receives each batch and bleaches only the first few levels ranging from level 3 to level 9.
2. The hair is then evaluated to see if it can reach Level 10, 11, or 12 safely
3. Based on results, production assigns batches to the appropriate color levels:
    *   Strong hair → Natural shades, red shades, darker colors
    *   Weak hair → Levels 10-12
### **Why This Matters:**
*   Each batch is unique based on natural processing response
*   Lighter colors cost more (more processing, labor, careful handling)
*   Some variations occur because natural hair responds differently

# "Bleach" Designation

### **Why We Use "Bleach" in Names:**
*   Sets expectations about processed nature and characteristics
*   Let's the stylist know further lightening or dyeing may be possible as the hair has not been dyed

# Texture-Specific Color Limitations

### **Natural Wave & Extra Body Wave:**
*   Available in ALL levels 1-12
*   Can handle full processing range
*   May show slightly relaxed texture at lightest levels
### **Curly Hair:**
*   Available ONLY in Levels 1-11 Bleach
*   **Level 11 Ash and 12 is NOT available and never will be**
*   Extreme processing destroys protein bonds that create curl patterns
*   Non-negotiable limitation based on hair science
### **Level 11 Bleach Curly Achievement:**
*   Recently accomplished through production innovation
*   Significant progress requiring careful technique development
*   Shows noticeably looser curls than darker levels
*   Customers must accept looser curl definition for lighter color

* * *

# Natural Color Variations

**Batch-to-Batch Differences:** Each batch starts with slightly different natural color and responds uniquely to processing.
### **Why Variations Occur:**
*   Different starting points (some darker, some lighter Level 2)
*   Individual strand response varies (genetic structure, condition)
*   Some strands lighten faster, others resist longer
**Acceptable Range:** Each level has an acceptable range rather than exact specification. Companies promising perfect consistency are chemically standardizing or coating to mask variations. Our variations are evidence of authenticity.

# Color and Hair Quality

### **Darker Colors (1-6) = Healthier Hair:**
*   Minimally processed, maximum structural integrity
*   Longest lasting, best performance
*   Best choice for prioritizing longevity and health
### **Mid-Range Colors (7-9) = Good Balance:**
*   Substantial processing but most hair maintains good quality
*   Sweet spot: noticeable lightening without extreme damage risks
*   Popular range for balanced results
### **Lightest Colors (10-12) = Most Compromised:**
*   Extreme processing inevitably compromises structure
*   More delicate, potentially higher porosity
*   Requires intensive care
*   Prioritizing color over hair health
*   Trade-offs in texture, durability, maintenance
### **Color Longevity:**
*   Levels 1-7: Begin fading after ~1 year (impressive longevity from textile dye process)
*   Levels 8-12: May fade somewhat faster due to compromised structure
*   All colors last many months before noticeable fading

# Special Considerations

### **Custom Color Matching:**
*   Requires photos in natural lighting (bright daylight, preferably outdoors)
*   No filters or editing
*   Multiple angles needed
*   Artificial lighting distorts color perception
**Monitor Screens Vary:** Colors displayed on website won't look identical on every device. Screen settings, brightness, and calibration affect appearance. Don't rely solely on website photos—order swatches or request actual hair photos for accurate assessment.
**Lighting Changes Color Perception:** Same hair looks different in fluorescent, natural daylight, and incandescent lighting. Extensions will appear slightly different in various environments—this is normal color behavior, not a defect.
**Color Fading is Normal:** All hair color fades gradually over time (washing, sun, environmental factors). Our textile dye process resists fading better than most, but some fading is inevitable after 12+ months. This proves hair is lasting long enough to experience normal wear—actually positive.
## Understanding Shades vs. Levels
**Level = Depth/Darkness** (1-12 scale) Measures how dark or light the color is, without indicating tone or undertone.
**Shade = Undertone/Hue** Describes whether color leans warm (golden, red) or cool (ash, neutral).
**Both Specifications Needed:** Complete color requires both: "Level 7 Ash" or "Level 4 Natural"
*   Level alone is incomplete information
*   Same level, different shades = completely different appearance

# Our Shade Categories

### Natural Shades
**Characteristics:**
*   Warm undertones with golden richness
*   Subtle, sophisticated warmth (not brassy or orange)
*   Golden reflects in light, creates dimension
*   Most common and popular shades
**Available:** All levels 1-12 (most versatile category)
### Ash Shades
**Characteristics:**
*   Cool undertones such as green and blue with NO warmth, gold, or red
*   Sometimes appear grayish or silver-toned
*   Matte or slightly metallic appearance
**Available:** In levels 2-9 & 11 & 12 NOT available in level 10 or 1
### Red Shades
**Characteristics:**
*   Red undertones ranging from subtle auburn to vibrant red
*   Rich, saturated, multifaceted color
*   Creates dimension and visual interest
**Available:** in levels 2-7 only
**Note:** Prone to fading (red molecules escape hair shaft easily).
### Level 7 Gold
**Our ONLY Gold Shade:**
*   Only offered at Level 7 (no other levels)
*   More intensely golden than Level 7 Natural
*   Maximum warm gold saturation
*   Warm blonde without going to Level 8+
### Gray/White Special Category
Gray White and Ash White are special categories outside the standard 1-12 system. See Specialty Colors section for details.
