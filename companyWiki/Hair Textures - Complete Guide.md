# Natural Wave

**Overview:** Classic S-shaped wave patterns, most versatile texture
### **Wave Pattern:**
*   **Premium (Double Drawn):** 2A classification - subtle waves, weight pulls pattern down
*   **Layered (Single Drawn):** 2B classification - pronounced S-waves with more body
### **Available In:**
*   Both finishes: Premium AND Layered (only texture with both options fully stocked)
*   All colors: Levels 1-12, including specialty colors
*   Extension types: Cylinder I-tips, poly bonds; Hand-tied wefts are only available in Layered with our stock - premium can be custom ordered
### **Best For:**
*   **Premium:** Brand transitions (Great Lengths, Bellami), normal/thick hair, maximum fullness, wig makers
*   **Layered:** Fine/fragile hair, naturally wispy ends, natural appearance, budget-conscious clients
### **Key Differences:**
*   Premium: Fuller, blunter ends, heavier weight, softer waves (2A), +40-50% cost
*   Layered: Wispy ends, lighter weight, more pronounced waves (2B), standard pricing
* * *

# Extra Body Wave (EBW)

**Overview:** Beach wave/mermaid hair with full body from root to tip
**Wave Pattern:** 2C classification - zig-zag pattern, tighter and more defined than natural wave
### **Available In:**
*   **Layered finish ONLY** (fully stocked)
*   Premium available through custom order only - loses characteristic full-body appearance
*   All colors: Levels 1-12
*   All extension types, including micro weft track (standard stock)
### **Best For:**
*   Creating dimension when mixed with natural wave or curly
*   Matching "Irish curl" patterns (wavy + curly sections)
*   Clients wanting more drama than natural wave without full curly maintenance
*   Transitioning between straight and curly textures
*   Heat styling (holds curls better than natural wave)
### **Care Requirements:**
*   More frizz-prone than natural wave (more surface area)
*   Cannot be brushed completely smooth without creating frizz
*   Requires wave-enhancing products and anti-frizz control
*   Should not be over-manipulated or excessively brushed
### **Important Notes:**
*   Most versatile texture for blending
*   Can be worn naturally wavy or heat-styled
*   "On the verge of curl" - temperamental about handling

# Curly Hair

**Overview:** Ringlet-forming curls, highest maintenance texture
### **Available Types:**
*   **3A:** Loose curls, ~1.5" ringlet separation (stocked)
*   **3B:** Medium curls, ~1" ringlet separation (stocked)
*   **3C:** Tight curls, <0.5" ringlet separation (custom order only - requires client photos)
**ONLY Available in Layered Finish:** Premium curly is NOT offered and never will be - weight destroys curl definition
### **Color Availability:**
*   Levels 1-11 Bleach only
*   NO Level 12 (destroys curl integrity)
*   NO Level 11 Ash dyed
*   Darker colors maintain tighter curls; lighter colors show looser curls due to bleaching effects
### **Length & Shrinkage:**
*   Standard stock: 16"-20" stretched measurement
*   Custom orders: 12"-30"
*   **Shrinkage:** 2-3" average (15-20%)
    *   16" stretched = ~14-14.5" relaxed appearance
    *   Longer lengths show more absolute shrinkage
*   **Landing Length:** For custom orders, specify the desired relaxed appearance length
### **Extension Availability:**
*   All types: Cylinder I-tips, poly bonds, hand-tied wefts
*   Wefts require even longer source hair due to construction + shrinkage

## Curly Hair Care (CRITICAL)

### **Maintenance Reality:**
*   **Highest maintenance** of all textures - constant, daily work
*   Requires significant time, effort, and product investment
*   Not everyone can sustain the commitment
### **Essential Rules:**
1. **NEVER brush when dry** - destroys curl definition, creates massive frizz
2. Only detangle when soaking wet with conditioner/product
3. Wash only 2-3x per week maximum (co-wash between shampoos)
4. Clarify every 2-3 weeks to remove product buildup
5. Constant moisture needed - daily or near-daily application
### **Required Products:**
*   Moisturizing shampoo
*   Lightweight conditioner
*   Leave-in conditioner
*   Curl cream
*   Hair oil/serum
*   Anti-frizz products
*   **Bleached curly needs:** Bond booster & intensive spray treatment
### **Styling Method:**
1. Apply all products to soaking wet hair
2. Scrunch upward (cup hair in palms, squeeze toward scalp)
3. Dry with diffuser OR air dry (don't touch while drying)
4. Optional: Use pick for root lift once completely dry
5. Never drag combs/brushes through dry curls
### **Reality Check:**
*   Curly hair is a daily routine that becomes part of your life
*   Constant battle against dryness, frizz, and tangling
*   Some clients eventually give up and straighten for simplicity
*   Consider EBW if not willing to commit to intensive care
*   Worth it for genuinely committed clients who love the look

# Texture Mixing Guidelines

### **Best Combinations:**
*   **Curly + EBW:** Most successful mix, replicates "Irish curl" patterns naturally
*   **Natural Wave + EBW:** Creates dimension, matches natural texture variations
### **Avoid:**
*   **Curly + Natural Wave:** Too much contrast, obvious separation (use EBW as a bridge)
### **Why EBW is the Bridge:**
*   Sits between natural wave (2A-2B) and curly (3A-3B) on texture spectrum
*   Blends successfully with textures on either side
*   Creates a graduated texture change, not abrupt seperation
### **When Mixing is Needed:**
*   Irish curl patterns (genetic texture variation)
*   Texture changes from hormones, bleaching, medical conditions
*   Creating natural dimension and preventing "wiggy" uniform appearance

# Critical Reminders

1. **All hair is same quality source** - difference is finish and natural texture
2. **Premium ≠ Better** - it's different, not superior (choose based on needs)
3. **Color affects texture** - lighter colors = looser curls/waves due to bleaching
4. **Curly requires commitment** - don't underestimate maintenance demands
5. **Shrinkage is normal** - always specify stretched vs. relaxed length expectations
6. **3C custom orders require photos** - submit wash-day pics showing the back of the head for Production approval
7. **EBW is most versatile** - successfully bridges between wave and curl textures

# **Why Straight Hair Dominates the Market**

### **Comfort Factor for Stylists**
"Straight hair has become the norm. Stylists are scared of textured hair." Most stylists have extensive training and experience with straight hair but minimal exposure to textured hair care.
This comfort level disparity makes stylists default to straight hair recommendations even when textured hair would better match the client's natural texture.
### **Common Stylist Concerns**
**"Should I straighten the hair first, then install, then style naturally?"** This question reveals a fundamental misunderstanding of textured hair installation.
**"How much density do I use? It changes with texture."** Stylists struggle with the reality that textured hair installation differs from straight hair protocols.
**"I don't know how to work with this."** Many stylists lack confidence working with textures they didn't learn during training.
### **Knowledge Gap**
The hair extension industry has focused primarily on straight hair education, leaving stylists underprepared for textured hair applications. Training programs emphasize straight hair installation with textured hair as an afterthought.
This educational gap creates stylist hesitation around textured hair. They don't want to attempt applications they're uncertain about, so they steer clients toward familiar straight hair.
### **Nicole's Educational Role**
Nicole spends significant time educating stylists that textured hair is manageable when you understand it. She explains density differences, styling approaches, and maintenance requirements specific to textured extensions.
This education gradually builds stylist confidence with textured hair. As more stylists become comfortable, textured extensions will become more common in the market.
### **Gray Area Status**
"Even with textured hair being more popular and people embracing natural textures, it's still a gray area. Not everybody's fully educated." The trend toward natural texture hasn't yet translated to full industry competence.
We're in a transitional period where demand for textured hair is growing faster than stylist education can keep pace. This creates opportunities for educated stylists who invest in textured hair expertise.
