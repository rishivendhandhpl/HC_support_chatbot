### How is your hair compared to other extension brands?
Our hair is different than all the other extension brands. Our hair can't be treated like all 100% Non-Remy (non-cuticle) Asian silicone-based extensions from your typical big name brands on the market. Our hair is 100% Indian Fine Natural Remy Cuticle hair and not coated in silicone. We own our own production house and work very closely with our suppliers to provide our stylists with the utmost hair quality. Anything done to this hair goes directly on the hair fibers and is not protected by silicone. Meaning there is a high risk things can go wrong when chemically altering — alter at your own risk! If you have clients who like to blow out their hair every day, heat style, and wash them every day; might not be the right fit with us. As all our hair is naturally wavy and curly and will never lay flat or pin straight — since Indian textures do not come like that. As well, all those things ruin the longevity of the hair and potentially have your client reaching out with hair issues fast unless they are used to the nature of our natural textures. Real Indian Remy Cuticle hair has a lot of waves and body to it giving that natural look & feel. This is why texture plays a huge part in selecting the right extensions for your client with us. We like to be transparent with all our customers, as the number one mistake our new customers do is assuming this hair will behave just like all other extensions on the market.

### What does 100% Remy mean?
Any good quality extensions should be made from 100% real hair, but that doesn't always imply it is 100% Remy. Non-Remy hair has roots and ends going in both directions and requires acid washing to remove the top layers of the cuticles or the hair will tangle severely. Following the acid wash, the hair is coated with silicone creating almost doll-like hair. Remy hair extensions will have the roots at the top with all cuticles intact and running in the same direction. Great care is made to maintain root direction to avoid tangling. It is far more challenging to produce but worth the result of natural-looking and feeling hair.

### Can you help me color match?

### When a color level displays Natural, Ash, Gold, Bleach or Red. What does that mean?
Colors are described by number levels. For H&C we use levels 1-12. Each level has a certain hue or tone to it. For example, 7 Gold means the hair is bleach washed and dyed to a level 7 and has a Gold hue/tone to it. We also abbreviate colors for dual tone extensions. For example, 7G/10N Blend translates to 7 Gold / 10 Natural 50/50 Blend (side-by-side). The hue/tones we offer are: N = Natural, B = Bleach, A = Ash, G = Gold, R = Red. NOTE: we do not offer every hue/tone in every level.

### What is a Blend? What is a Rooted?
**For Poly and Cylinder:** Blends are equal amounts of two colors side-by-side within each strand of the Poly and Cylinder extension. Different combinations of 2-color blends are available in Layered, Premium and Curly, Large tips only. Rooted are a darker root color fading down to a body color, often creating a third middle color along the fade. Lengths of 16 and 18 inches will have a 1 inch root fading down to 4 inches. Lengths of 20 and 22 inches will have a 2 inch root fading down to 6 inches. **For Hand-Tied Wefts:** Blends are 1/4 inch streaks of two colors alternating along the weft track. Different combinations of 2-color blends are available in Layered and Curly, excluding the 14 inch length weft extensions. Rooted options follow the same fade patterns as Poly and Cylinder.

### Does the hair Oxidize?
Hair color does oxidize over time, usually due to water minerals and sometimes the type of hair care that harshly scrubs the surface of the hair strands, thereby removing dye particles. If you have hard water, it can increase oxidation. Usually, hard water treatment can help immensely, as sold by Malibu C. To get rid of excess build-up that adds yellow and orange hues, we also recommend clarifying shampoo wash followed by a very diluted purple shampoo wash. We recommend using a low pH conditioner to finish and close the cuticles making the hair soft and smooth again.

## Textures & Product Lines

### Do you have straight hair?
Our hair texture ranges from a slight body wave, full body wave, & curly as is the natural texture of Indian hair. Every batch of hair will have variations of texture and strand density. Natural Indian hair texture is not straight. If a client wants a smooth straight style, the hair will need to be trained and properly styled. This means the hair will not naturally lay thick and heavy, nor will it dry straight like most Asian hair blends. Cuticle hair with a natural texture will behave exactly like a normal head of hair. If you desire a straight hairstyle, first blow dry the hair until it is completely dry. Then pass over the hair once or twice with a flat iron. The hair will be smooth and soft from root to tip. DO NOT USE HOT TOOLS ON DAMP HAIR, AS THIS WILL CAUSE SIGNIFICANT DAMAGE. It is also recommended to apply heat protectants when straightening blond hair colors.

### What are the different hair textures you offer?
Our hair textures are: **Premium (Double Drawn 70%)** — This means more long strands than short strands, creating a slight body wave at the bottom of the hair with fullness at the ends. Only available in Cylinder (I-tip) and Poly (K-Tip) ready-made extensions. **Layered (Single Drawn)** — The hair is made up of short strands and long strands. Short strands typically start at 6-8 inches, creating a Body Wave with thinner and wispy ends. Available in all extension methods. **Extra Body Wave** — Also single-drawn, creating a Full Body Wave. Short strands start from beginning to end, like a beach wave, super wavy throughout. Available in all extension methods. **Curly** — 100% natural curly single drawn hair that forms ringlets. Tightness or looseness depends on donors, batch blend, color and length. Available in all extension methods. Max bleach level for curly is level 10.

### What's the difference between Premium and Layered?
All of our hair is of the same quality and from the same source. The difference is how we finish the types of hair and the prices. Premium Hair is 70% Double Drawn with only the bottom a few inches tapering to the ends, creating a smoother, softer wave. This is for clients who have fuller ends but also want some waves. Layered Hair is Single Drawn with short strands starting 6 inches from the top, tapering down and creating a full-body wave. This is for clients that want lots of body and texture with natural ends.

### How long does your hair last?
Our hair can last up to a year with the proper care; we do have customers who have worn the extensions up to 3 years! Levels typically 1 through 8 will last up to a year, and levels 9-12 will last up to 6 months. The key to long-lasting extensions is moisturizing and low heat styling. Depending on the method used, Hand-Tied Weft and Cylinder is 1-3 years & Polymer Bonds last 3-4 months. Also, following our Maintenance Guide and Maintenance Blogs help greatly!

### How much do I need for a Full head? Partial fill?
For a **Full head**, we recommend 4-6 ounces/120-180 grams on average. For Wefts, this is 4-6 bundles. For Poly or Cylinder, this is 7-10 bundles. This is for thicker haired clients or those looking for length and volume. For a **Partial fill**, we recommend 2-3 ounces/60-90 grams on average. For Wefts, this is 2-3 bundles. For Poly or Cylinder, this is 4-6 bundles. This is for thinner hair clients or those wanting a little bit of volume.

## Curly Hair

### How do you measure Curly hair?
We measure our Curly hair both relaxed and pulled straight. When relaxed and curly the hair may be 1 inch less up to the exact length stated. When pulled straight the hair will be at least 1 inch longer. For example, 16 inch Curly hair will be 15-16 inches relaxed and 17 inches pulled straight.

### Is your Curly hair permed?
Our curly hair is natural. We don't use perms. All of our hair is a natural Indian texture and comes from donors with naturally wavy hair or a natural loose curl. The processes we apply are bleaching the hair to a lighter level and depositing fiber dyes to achieve the shades we offer. The lighter the color level, the less wave or curl because bleaching breaks the disulfide bonds and reshapes the natural texture. The lightest level for Curly hair is level 10.

### Do you have a selection of curl types?
All of our hair is naturally curly; it can range in length and curl depending upon the donors, the lengths, and the color process. Every batch is unique, as all the hair for our extensions comes from different heads, therefore curl patterns will never be the same and will always vary. Specific curl patterns can be requested but only through a Custom Order. Another thing to remember, the longer the hair the looser the curl, OR the lighter the color the more loose the curl.

### Is frizz normal with Curls?
Yes! Natural curly hair is frizzy and fluffy. To define the curl and avoid being so fluffy, you must hydrate & style it with some styling products and diffuse the curl. This is why we make it crucial the person wearing our curls match the texture before application and make sure they understand the wear and care of naturally curly hair.

### How curly does it get?
The tightness or looseness of the curl depends on the donors, how we batch blend, color and length. The roots to mid-way tend to be stretched out due to the weight of the hair. The middle to ends can be 1 inch ringlets to 3 inch deep waves. We leave it to production to mix and match donor bundles that become a certain color and length batch, so we often do not know what will be the final result until we receive the hair. All the curly hair is washed, brushed, shaken out and then hung up to dry before we label and stock the hair. You are always welcome to request photos of a particular color/length before placing an order.

### Do the curls lose their form after a while?
Naturally curly hair will not lose curl like permed hair. The only way to change the body and texture of the hair is to permanently break the disulfide bonds, which can be done with chemicals or repeated application of heat.

### What lengths do you carry in Curly?
We only stock our curly hair in specific lengths. If you do not see the length you need, you'll have to consider a custom order. The following lengths are offered in Curly: Hand-Tied Wefts — 16 inch & 20 inch. Polymer Bond — 16 inch & 18 inch. Cylinder I-Tip — 16 inch & 18 inch.

### Would I be able to get your Curly hair in a level 11-12?
Our curly hair line is not available in colors lighter than a level 10. Naturally curly hair will not hold their curl when bleaching and tend to lose all curls beyond a level 10 bleaching process. We appreciate your understanding.

## Quality Notes

### I received my order and the hair has a mark across the bundles near the top, what is it?
On occasion you may see a line across the hair about 6 inches from the top of the extensions. This is a watermark created when we wrap the top of the extensions prior to washing, tangle testing and conditioning of new batches. Every single bundle is washed and tested. When we remove the plastic cover from the top of the bundles and let the hair hang dry, it often creates a watermark. The watermark disappears with the first wash when all the hair has been saturated with water.
